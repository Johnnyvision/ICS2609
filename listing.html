Group A

Group A consists of two-seater cars, also known as sports cars or roadsters, are designed for maximum driving enjoyment with an emphasis on style, speed, and handling. These vehicles are distinguished by a low-slung profile, sleek body lines, and a powerful engine that provides a thrilling driving experience.

Group B

Group B consists of four-seater vehicles, also known as sedans or saloons, are built for comfort, convenience, and practicality. These vehicles typically have a roomy interior with seating for four passengers and a trunk for luggage or storage.

Group C

Group C consists of SUVs (Sport Utility Vehicles) are versatile vehicles that combine comfort, space, and off-road capability. When compared to other types of cars, these vehicles typically have a higher ground clearance, a stronger body frame, and a larger cargo capacity.

Group D

Group D consists of Vans, which are vehicles that are primarily used to transport goods or a large number of passengers. These vehicles are typically designed with a high roof and a large interior, providing plenty of space for cargo or passengers.
